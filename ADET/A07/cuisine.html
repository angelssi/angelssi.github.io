The Art of Japanese Cuisine
Japanese cuisine is a rich and diverse culinary tradition known for its emphasis on fresh, seasonal ingredients, balance, and aesthetic presentation. Rice is the cornerstone of most meals, often accompanied by fish (raw, grilled, or fried), seafood, and vegetables. Sushi and sashimi, featuring vinegared rice and thinly sliced raw fish, are internationally recognized staples. Noodles like ramen, udon, and soba also play a key role, while tempura (battered and fried vegetables or seafood) and tofu add variety. Japanese sweets (wagashi), made from ingredients like red bean paste and matcha, offer a sweet balance to the savory dishes, and green tea remains a cultural staple.
