Why Visit Sunshine Farm Philippines?

Empowers PWDs Through Hope

Sunshine Farm is more than just a farm; it is an opportunity to support a meaningful cause. Dedicated to employing Persons with Disabilities (PWDs), the farm provides sustainable livelihoods and empowers individuals to overcome societal stigmas.Sunshine Farm fosters an inclusive environment, offering equal opportunities and uplifting spirits, making every visit a rewarding and impactful experience.

A Haven for Sunflower Lovers

Sunshine Farm is a perfect place for sunflower lovers and anyone who enjoys nature. The owner, a true sunflower fan, has filled the farm with beautiful blooms and sunflower-inspired designs. Even if you’re not a sunflower lover, you can still appreciate the lovely scenery and peaceful atmosphere. Sunshine Farm offers a delightful experience for everyone. Sunshine Farm is for anyone who loves nature.

Supports United Nations Sustainable Development Goals

Sunshine Farm is dedicated to the UN Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) 5, 8, 10, and 15. The farm creates job opportunities for individuals with disabilities of all genders, promoting equality and inclusivity. Additionally, by cultivating flowers and enhancing the environment, Sunshine Farm plays a vital role in promoting biodiversity and preserving local ecosystems. It helps us to have a sustainable future.

Embraces Filipino Tradition of Pasalubong

Sunshine Farm embraces the Filipino tradition of pasalubong, offering a variety of affordable, handcrafted souvenirs made by individuals with disabilities (PWDs) in our Souvenir Shop. You can also find a selection of plants and our exclusive "Tita Sunshine Products". Every purchase supports our mission to provide sustainable employment for more PWDs, making your gifts meaningful and impactful.

About Us


Rhodora Palomar-Fresnedi is passionate about two things: helping people with disabilities (PWDs) and growing flowers. In the past, she has helped PWDs find jobs, but she wanted to do more. With many ideas in mind, she wasn't sure where to begin until she met Mike Caballes, a sunflower expert. This meeting sparked an idea—why not start a sunflower farm that PWDs can manage?

Two weeks after meeting Mike, Rhodora began planting sunflower seeds on a vacant lot in Lalig, Quezon. She hired two PWDs to help, and in just 45 days, the flowers bloomed beautifully, attracting butterflies, bees, and even fireflies at night. The farm quickly gained attention, with people stopping by to buy sunflowers. Rhodora's first harvest was a success, and she received more orders than she could handle.

Now, Rhodora is focused on making the farm sustainable, allowing her to continue providing jobs for PWDs. She has expanded her team to eight PWD employees and is preparing for an even larger farm. Her goal is clear: she’s not just selling sunflowers; she’s creating jobs for PWDs. Sunshine Farm Philippines is all about planting hope, where hope for PWDs means having a job and a future.
